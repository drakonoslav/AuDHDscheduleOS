Why this is the right geometry

Your meals are not phases themselves.

They are better understood as:
	•	slots
	•	which have phase tendencies
	•	within a larger plan family

So:
	•	precardio is a slot
	•	base or carbup is the nutritional regime
	•	priming, loading, etc. is the physiological interpretation

That is much cleaner.

Suggested phase translation by meal slot

Here is the cleanest first-pass mapping.

Universal slot meaning

1. precardio → Priming

Because it is light, activating, and low-burden.

Example:
	•	banana

This is clearly:
	•	readiness
	•	wake-up
	•	low digestive drag
	•	movement preparation

2. postcardio → Priming or Loading

Because it begins substrate restoration and sets the day.

Your oat + whey + MCT meal is still pretty controlled, so in base mode it leans Priming.
In carb-up mode it leans more Loading.

3. midmorning → Partitioning or Expression

This is more of a stabilizing anchor meal.
It is not explosive.
It helps keep the day routed correctly.

Your yogurt + flax + whey meal feels like:
	•	steady
	•	controlled
	•	not highly anabolic-spiky
	•	routing/maintenance oriented

So default lean:
	•	Partitioning

4. prelift → Loading

This one is the easiest.
It is explicit fuel input before major tension work.

Dextrin + whey is almost a pure loading signal.

5. postlift → Accumulation or Rebound

This depends on how you define the window.

I would treat immediate postlift nutrition as:
	•	Accumulation if the emphasis is absorbing the training burden
	•	Rebound if the emphasis is supercompensation recovery

For your system, defaulting it to Accumulation is cleaner, because it belongs to the stimulus block before the later recovery arc resolves.

6. evening → Resensitization or Expression

Your evening meal is restorative and grounding:
	•	eggs
	•	flax
	•	MCT
	•	oats
	•	banana

That leans:
	•	nervous system settling
	•	overnight restoration
	•	replenishment without acute performance demand

So default:
	•	Resensitization

Small but important insight

Your sleep phases and meal phases do not have to align one-to-one.

They should align as:
	•	parallel interpreters of the same day-state

Meaning:
	•	sleep may classify the day as resensitization
	•	nutrition may be deployed in a loading pattern
	•	training may still sit in accumulation

That is not a bug. That is actually richer and more biologically believable.

So I would avoid trying to force every subsystem to one identical label all the time.

Instead, use:
	•	sleep_phase
	•	nutrition_phase
	•	training_phase
	•	day_master_phase

Then the app can reason about cross-system mismatch.
