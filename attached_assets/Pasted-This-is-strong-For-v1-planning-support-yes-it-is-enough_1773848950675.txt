The missing layer

You have:
	•	state trends
	•	nutrition phases
	•	training logs

What you do not yet have is an explicit:

Training Readiness Interpreter

Something like:
	•	compound_readiness
	•	volume_tolerance
	•	coordination_readiness
	•	recovery_margin

Without that, the app can show trends, but it still has to leap too far to say:

“do compounds today”

Because compounds are not just “good day / bad day.”

They depend on:
	•	CNS readiness
	•	clarity
	•	stability
	•	confidence
	•	coordination
	•	fatigue burden
	•	sleep architecture
	•	sometimes hormone signal too

So is the current trending enough?

For general planning: yes

For meal recommendations: yes, cautiously

For nuanced hypertrophy training selection: not fully yet

That is the honest line.

Why meal recommendations are closer than lifting recommendations

Meal recommendations are easier because they can follow cleaner logic like:
	•	high recovery need + low HRV trend → avoid underfueling
	•	repeated poor pre-lift motivation + pre-lift meal outside window → shift timing
	•	high sensory load + low appetite consistency → use safer, simpler templates
	•	certain nutrition phases correlating with more recovery-favoring days → review whether that phase is too aggressive

That is already within reach.

So yes:

this trending should lead to meal template recommendations

That is a very natural next step.

Why lifting recommendations are harder

To advise:
	•	compounds
	•	compounds but lighter
	•	isolation focus
	•	deload style
	•	performance push

the app needs a specific classifier.

Because a day can look like:
	•	ADHD pull high
	•	HRV mediocre
	•	recovery need low
	•	sensory load moderate
	•	motivation high
	•	mental clarity not great

That may mean:
	•	not ideal for maximal compound execution
	•	but perfectly fine for bodybuilding isolation and machine work

So the missing question is not:

“Can I train?”

It is:

“What kind of training stress is best supported today?”

That requires a layer above raw trends.

What I would add next

Not more charts.

Add a small derived engine:

Training Mode Recommendation

	•	compound_performance_favoring

Example logic

Compound-performance favoring

Needs something like:
	•	recovery need low
	•	HRV stable/up
	•	RHR stable/down
	•	mental clarity decent
	•	physical energy decent
	•	not high sensory load

Hypertrophy-volume favoring

Allows:
	•	okay but not elite readiness
	•	decent motivation
	•	acceptable recovery
	•	enough fuel support
	•	training can be productive without requiring top-end sharpness

Isolation-stability favoring

Use when:
	•	energy is okay
	•	clarity or CNS sharpness is not ideal
	•	sensory load is higher
	•	you still want productive muscle stimulus

This is probably the category you’re describing when you say:

maybe not particularly strong that day

Recovery-training favoring

Use when:
	•	recovery need high
	•	HRV declining
	•	RHR rising
	•	poor sleep trend
	•	overload accumulating
