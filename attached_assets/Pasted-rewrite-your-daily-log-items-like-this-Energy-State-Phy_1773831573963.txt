The biggest improvement: use “felt statement” wording

Instead of abstract labels alone, each item can open from a sentence stem.

For example:
	•	Physical energy → “My body feels…”
	•	Motivation → “Starting things feels…”
	•	Mental fog → “My thinking feels…”
	•	Novelty hunger → “I want today to feel…”
	•	Structure hunger → “I need today to be…”
	•	Noise aversion → “Noise feels…”

That makes scoring much easier.

Example:

Structure hunger

“My need for predictability today feels…”
	•	1 = low
	•	3 = moderate
	•	5 = very high

That is much more natural than just seeing the label.

One subtle but important suggestion

Try not to make yourself distinguish too much between 4 and 5.

A good rule is:
	•	1 = clearly low
	•	2 = somewhat low
	•	3 = medium
	•	4 = high
	•	5 = very high / dominant

You do not need perfect precision.
You need repeatable self-judgment.

⸻

My strongest recommendation

For each scale, define a hidden check:

“Would I notice this today without being asked?”

If no, it is probably 1–2.
If yes, it is probably 3.
If it is shaping the day, it is 4–5.

That makes the ratings much more intuitive.
