Why each one maps that way

base → Expression

Base is your stabilized operating baseline:
	•	not pushing hard
	•	not cutting hard
	•	not resetting hard

That sounds most like:

Expression = system stabilized at higher baseline

Secondary fit is Priming if you want base to mean “ready, calm, baseline readiness,” but overall Expression is better.

⸻

carbup → Loading

Carb-up means:
	•	fuel increase
	•	rising training support
	•	more tension support
	•	more input pressure

That fits:

Loading = force production rising

If carb-up becomes very aggressive and sustained, it can drift toward Accumulation.

⸻

peakbulk → Saturation

Peak bulk is your most aggressive growth push:
	•	highest carb pressure
	•	highest surplus
	•	strongest hypertrophy drive
	•	highest fatigue/fat-gain risk

That is closest to:

Saturation = maximum cellular stress

Secondary fit: Accumulation if you want a slightly less extreme interpretation.

⸻

recomp → Partitioning

Recomp is not mainly about max input.
It is about:
	•	routing nutrients well
	•	preserving/building muscle while controlling fat
	•	efficient allocation

That is almost exactly:

Partitioning = direct nutrients toward muscle vs fat

Secondary fit: Expression, since recomp can also feel like a stable, controlled state.

⸻

carbcut → Partitioning

Carb cut means:
	•	less fuel availability
	•	more careful allocation
	•	less “push,” more routing control

So it fits:

Partitioning

If carb reduction is substantial and meant to restore responsiveness, it moves toward Resensitization.

⸻

fatcut → Partitioning

Same logic as carbcut, but even more clearly:
	•	this is an allocation/routing regime
	•	reduce excess storage pressure
	•	improve nutrient direction

Best fit is again:

Partitioning

Secondary fit: Resensitization if the cut is also functioning as a reset phase.

⸻

deload → Resensitization

This is the cleanest one.

Deload means:
	•	reduced strain
	•	restored sensitivity
	•	restored CNS and tissue response
	•	technique and recovery emphasis

That is directly:

Resensitization

Secondary fit: Priming, because once recovery happens the system becomes ready again.

⸻

dietbreak → Rebound

Diet break is not just “eating more.”
Its role is usually:
	•	relieve adaptive suppression
	•	restore performance
	•	restore output
	•	reduce chronic deficit drag

That best fits:

Rebound = supercompensation expression

Secondary fit: Expression if you use diet break more as stabilization than recovery surge.

The only awkward part

This leaves:
	•	Priming
	•	Accumulation

without direct old-label equivalents.

That is because your old system is regime-oriented, not fully cyclical.

Those two are “missing” because:
	•	Priming is more of an entry/readiness state than a diet label
	•	Accumulation is more of a stimulus-build state than a broad nutrition label

Best inferred additions

If you want to conceptually place them:
	•	Priming = pre-base / entry into build
	•	Accumulation = late carbup / early peakbulk bridge

So a fuller orbital progression from your current labels would be:
	1.	Priming → not explicitly represented
	2.	Loading → carbup
	3.	Accumulation → between carbup and peakbulk
	4.	Saturation → peakbulk
	5.	Partitioning → recomp / carbcut / fatcut
	6.	Resensitization → deload
	7.	Rebound → dietbreak
	8.	Expression → base

That is probably the most elegant translation.
