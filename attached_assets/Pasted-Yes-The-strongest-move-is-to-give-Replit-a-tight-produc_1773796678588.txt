The most important decision engine

This is the heart of the whole product.

Instead of asking “was the schedule followed,” ask:

Was the block aligned with my state?

That means each day the app should estimate:

Explorer pull

Higher when:
	•	novelty hunger high
	•	boredom high
	•	restlessness high
	•	ideation high
	•	urge to change plan high

Stabilizer pull

Higher when:
	•	structure hunger high
	•	sensory sensitivity high
	•	transition difficulty high
	•	ambiguity intolerance high
	•	routine craving high

Recovery pull

Higher when:
	•	overload high
	•	sleep poor
	•	social/sensory burden high
	•	resistance high across blocks
	•	drift + shutdown both present

⸻

Suggested daily modes

The app should recommend one of these before planning:

1. Expansion day

Use when explorer pull is high and overload is not too high.

Characteristics:
	•	idea capture block
	•	flexible work block
	•	optional novelty slot
	•	shorter rigid blocks
	•	more movement breaks

2. Structuring day

Use when stabilizer pull is high and overload is moderate/manageable.

Characteristics:
	•	repeated meals
	•	exact training times
	•	reduced options
	•	low-friction errands
	•	fewer transitions
	•	checklist-driven work

3. Recovery day

Use when overload or shutdown risk is high.

Characteristics:
	•	protected wake and bedtime
	•	repeated safe foods
	•	stripped-down to essentials
	•	low sensory errands or none
	•	easier cardio, maybe Zone 2
	•	lifting only if restorative, not heroic

Nutrition logic you want Replit to support

You are not looking for infinite meal variety. You want stable templates + adaptive timing.

So tell Replit to build:

Meal templates

Each template stores:
	•	meal name
	•	ingredients
	•	grams
	•	macros
	•	texture category
	•	prep demand
	•	sensory comfort level
	•	portability
	•	digestion heaviness
	•	sameness / predictability score

Example:
	•	Meal 1: oat flour + whey + MCT
	•	Meal 2: Greek yogurt + flax + whey
	•	Meal 3: cyclic dextrin + whey
	•	Meal 4: evening oats + eggs + banana

What the app should learn

For each meal, ask:
	•	easy to eat?
	•	delayed too long?
	•	too heavy before training?
	•	too light after training?
	•	felt grounding?
	•	caused avoidance?
	•	better earlier/later?

Then let the app suggest:
	•	move Meal 1 earlier by 20 min
	•	reduce pre-lift fiber/fat
	•	make post-cardio meal more automatic
	•	keep same foods but shift meal 3 closer to lift

That is much better than “eat different foods.”

Cardio and lifting logic

The app should not only track if you trained. It should track whether the placement worked.

For cardio:
	•	planned time
	•	actual time
	•	duration
	•	intensity
	•	before/after food
	•	effect on later focus
	•	effect on appetite
	•	effect on sensory calm
	•	effect on lift performance later

For lift:
	•	planned time
	•	actual time
	•	duration
	•	pre-lift meal timing
	•	post-lift meal timing
	•	motivation before
	•	resistance before
	•	satisfaction after
	•	later fatigue cost
	•	bedtime impact

Then the app can discover patterns like:
	•	lifting after too many errands causes high resistance
	•	cardio early improves structure for the whole day
	•	late lifting harms bedtime consistency
	•	pre-lift carbs 45–60 min prior work better than 15 min prior

Sensory fit
	•	Which environments raised overload most?

Nutrition fit
	•	Which meals were most reliable?
	•	Which meal timings improved training and calm?

Training fit
	•	When did cardio stabilize the day?
	•	When did lifting feel most sustainable?

Recommendation summary
	•	Keep
